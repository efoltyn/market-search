Case Study

Intel: Proof of Work

2026-01-18 • Eli

This is what “tool-first” analysis looks like in practice: a single prompt, a chain of commands, and a critical summary grounded in numbers and filings.

The command trail

Eli did not start with headlines. It resolved symbols, pulled prices, then fetched filings and news only after the move was visible:

eli finance search --query "Intel"
eli finance filings --ticker INTC --forms 8-K,10-K,10-Q --limit 5
eli finance filings --ticker INTC --forms 8-K --limit 1 --include-text
eli finance timeseries --tickers INTC --range 1y --granularity 1mo
eli finance timeseries --tickers INTC,AMD,NVDA --range 3y --granularity 3mo
eli finance news --ticker INTC --date 2025-12-26
Result: a critical gap analysis tied to the 8-K equity issuance, negative free cash flow, and a widening valuation gap vs. peers.

What was surfaced

INTC showed a sharp price recovery, but fundamentals lagged. The NVIDIA deal showed up in filings and price, but did not close gaps in cash flow or foundry execution.

Why this matters

The output is not a story. It is a chain of verifiable commands, raw data, and a summary that you can rerun or audit.
