## Token Compression Techniques

### Summarization
Summarization involves condensing the original text into a shorter version that captures the main points. This can be done using extractive or abstractive methods.

**Example**:
- Original: "Intel's stock surged 10.8% on Friday, January 9, 2026, following positive comments from former President Trump."
- Summarized: "Intel's stock surged 10.8% after Trump's positive comments."

### Paraphrasing
Paraphrasing involves rephrasing the original text to make it more concise while retaining the same meaning.

**Example**:
- Original: "Meta plans to cut around 10% of employees in its Reality Labs business, indicating a strategic shift in its operations."
- Paraphrased: "Meta to reduce Reality Labs workforce by 10%, signaling a strategic shift."

### Keyword Extraction
Keyword extraction involves identifying and using only the most important keywords to convey the message.

**Example**:
- Original: "Meta is launching a new 'Meta Compute' initiative to build gigawatt-scale capacity for AI infrastructure."
- Keywords: "Meta, Meta Compute, AI infrastructure, gigawatt-scale capacity"

### Structured Data
Using structured data formats like JSON or bullet points can help in reducing token count while making the information more accessible.

**Example**:
- Original: "Intel's stock has shown significant volatility over the past year, with a notable surge in recent months. The stock price has increased from around $18 in April 2025 to over $44 in January 2026."
- Structured: 
  - **Stock**: Intel
  - **Volatility**: Significant
  - **Price Range**: $18 (April 2025) to $44 (January 2026)
