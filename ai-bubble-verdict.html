05Two things that turned the regime against it

The reaction function inverted. Record blowouts are being sold — Micron (84.6% margins, sold out to 2028) round-tripped; Broadcom (records) −20%; Nvidia's beat-and-raise faded. FactSet Q1-26: beats rewarded just +1.1% on a record beat rate, misses punished −4.9% (~1.7× normal). All downside, no upside to good news — yet sentiment is fear (CNN F&G 25), not euphoria. Translation: a skeptical, de-risking crowd already wringing out the excess — a bubble being let down, not blown bigger.

The Fed put is gone, and AI is the macro now. June's dots flipped to hikes (markets price ~78% odds of zero 2026 cuts) as PCE reaccelerated to 4.1%. The cheap-money regime that inflated the trade has reversed. And there's no second engine: AI capex drove ~74% of Q1 GDP growth while the rest frayed (consumer sentiment −21%, housing −15%). So an AI-capex air-pocket is an economy-wide repricing — and the market already prices the timing: 2026 recession odds ~10%, but 2027 ~44%. A buildout the market expects to end.

06The forward spine — counting the deck

The market level discounts the expected forward flow of catalysts. Reframe the whole thing as catalyst inventory: the up-fuel is discretionary and depleting (cards an agent chooses to play — Trump peace tweets, Fed jawboning, CEO hype, buybacks, guidance), while the down-fuel is exogenous and recurring (CPI/PCE/jobs, the FOMC decision, earnings results, a credit event). Support is a finite hand; threats arrive on a schedule.

  • Big bullish cards already played (spent, in the price): the AI-capex commitments, the memory super-cycle, the Micron/Nvidia/Broadcom blowouts, the first Iran de-escalation.
  • The Fed card is unplayable — frozen by 4.1% inflation; the dots show hikes.
  • The peace card is being replayed (the June 29 "Iran wants to meet in Doha" headline) — second play, diminishing punch, against a market that already watched the first one round-trip. A replayed card is a thin-hand tell.
  • The one fat unplayed card — a genuine AI-revenue inflection that closes the ~$600B spend-vs-revenue gap — is also the exact binary that, if it disappoints, is the bear trigger.

Today's rip (June 29: AMAT +11%, KLAC +12%, Corning +16%, ALAB +16%, Cerebras +19%, AST SpaceMobile +21%, MSTR +13%) was a card-driven bounce — analyst-upgrade cards (BofA on Lam, an Intel double-upgrade) + the Micron read-through + the peace replay — and notice the frothiest cohort (space, crypto, the AI-IPO) led hardest. A relief rip on played cards, with the speculative tail in front. Thin discretionary up-deck, full exogenous down-calendar — that's the depletion asymmetry, and it's why the down-leg, when it comes, is fast.

07What actually breaks it — the tripwires

It is not breaking now — IG/HY tight, VIX 18, sentiment fear, core cash-rich. It breaks when the contained credit problem becomes a contagious one:

  1. CCC contagion into BB/IG. Today CCC widens alone. If it drags the BB/B transmission tranches and HY breaks above its March wides (~3.5%), the subset bubble goes systemic. The single most important chart to watch.
  2. A neocloud / SPV credit event. CoreWeave or Nebius failing to roll the 2026 GPU debt wall, an Oracle/data-center-ABS markdown, or a forced GPU-collateral re-mark. Burry's "fraud not flywheel," made real.
  3. An AI-capex guide-down, or a Fed hike. A hyperscaler cutting capex breaks the circular loop and (since AI ≈ 74% of GDP growth) reprices the whole economy; an actual hike removes the last support. The reaction function says even good guidance may not save it.

08Bottom line

The verdict, unhedged

Not an "AI bubble." A bubble in the financing of AI, on top of a real and largely-earned compute build. The leaders (Nvidia 21.7× fwd, Micron 15.6× and sold out to 2028) are cash machines, not concept stocks — categorically not 2000. The mania lives one layer down, in the GPU-collateralized, circular, off-balance-sheet debt funding the neoclouds and Oracle against hardware whose rental value has already fallen 50–70% — and that layer is already deflating (Oracle −40 to −57%, CoreWeave −41%, CCC the only spread widening).

The closest historical rhyme is dark fiber, not dot-com: the technology is real and the capacity gets used — but the compute, like the fiber, will be enjoyed by later owners, after the leveraged builders and their financiers are restructured. The demand is sold-out and pre-paid; the financing is the bubble.

So: ~61/100 — a real, contained, already-venting subset bubble, not a systemic mania — but with three things that make the tail fat: the reaction function has inverted (good news sold), the Fed put is gone, and AI capex now is the macro, so the break — if CCC contagions into IG, or a neocloud can't roll its debt — is an economy-wide event, and the calendar where it clusters is 2027. Watch the credit seam, not the index. The level is calm; the level is not the signal.
